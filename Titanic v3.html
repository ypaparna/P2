def add_sex_indicator(Sex):
    if (Sex == 'female'):
        Sex_indicator = 1
    else:
        Sex_indicator = 2
    return Sex_indicator

Statistical Analysis¶

Statistical analysis will be a good way to test and confirm our hypothesis Use the chi-square test for independence to determine whether there is a significant relationship between two categorical variables Significance level. Often, researchers choose significance levels equal to 0.01, 0.05, or 0.10; but any value between 0 and 1 can be used. Significance level for these tests will be 0.05 Null hypothesis - Passenger Sex has no influence on survival rate Alternate hypothesis -Survival rate is influenced by Passenger Sex

import scipy.stats as scs
tabulation = pd.crosstab([titanic['Survived']], titanic['Sex'])

Conclusion¶

Based on the analysis conducted above, and considering the titanic data set as a sample, the following conclusions can be drawn for the population (for similar incidents). 1. Females have a significantly better chance of survival over men. 2. Passengers of the 1st class have better chance of survival over 2nd class, and 2nd class passengers over 3rd class. 3. Children and seniors have the best and worst chances of survival respectively. Limitations: -The conclusions are based on one sample(titanic) dataset. Correlation of the variables in the analysis doesn't imply causation. -Only controlled experiments will lead to causation. -Age was missing for 177 records and it was filled with the mean Age. This might have caused a slight impact on the analysis/conclusions based on Age - Most of the titanic data is collected from survivors and there might be some bias in the information they provided. Of course dead people can't be surveyed for their information.
